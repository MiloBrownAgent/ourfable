# Our Fable 14-Day Proof Sprint Kit

Goal: collect real proof assets strong enough to use near decision moments.

## Asset types to collect

### 1. Short parent quote
Ideal length:
- 1 to 2 sentences

Best capture prompt:
- What made you reserve?
- What made this feel worth paying attention to?
- What felt different from just saving things on your phone?

Where it can be used:
- /reserve near CTA
- paid landing pages
- founder trust section
- emails

Quality bar:
- specific
- emotionally real
- not generic praise
- sounds like a real parent, not marketing copy

### 2. Contributor quote
Ideal length:
- 1 sentence

Best capture prompt:
- What did it feel like to leave something for this child?
- What made the prompt or experience easy enough to do?

Where it can be used:
- contributor participation explanation
- /reserve trust section
- /for-your-family
- warm ads/emails

Quality bar:
- shows participation felt meaningful
- ideally mentions writing, recording, or leaving something for later

### 3. Screenshot proof
Ideal format:
- tasteful cropped screenshot
- no clutter
- no sensitive info beyond what is approved

Best capture candidates:
- reserve confirmation reaction
- contributor confirmation state
- parent seeing first item in vault
- emotionally strong text exchange about the product

Where it can be used:
- dormant /reserve proof module
- warm landing pages
- founder note section

Quality bar:
- legible on mobile
- emotionally clear in under 3 seconds
- approved for use

### 4. Founder-backed proof block
Ideal length:
- 3 to 5 bullets

Best content:
- built by Dave and Amanda for their own family first
- private by design
- export anytime
- no app required for contributors
- early families are already contributing real memories

Where it can be used:
- /reserve trust section
- FAQ
- founder note
- pitch / partner material

Quality bar:
- concrete
- specific
- non-hype

### 5. Reserve / reaction snippets
Ideal length:
- 1 line each

Best examples:
- “This made me wish we had started earlier.”
- “I sent this to my mom immediately.”
- “This feels like something our child will actually keep.”

Where it can be used:
- ad copy testing
- proof wall
- email snippets

Quality bar:
- concise
- plausible
- approved
- emotionally credible

## Collection instructions
- ask right after strong reactions happen
- ask while language is fresh
- capture exact phrasing before polishing
- get explicit approval before publishing
- save source/context for every quote or screenshot

## Minimum publish standard
Do not publish proof unless it is:
- real
- approved
- specific
- legible
- on-brand

## Priority order
1. parent quotes
2. contributor quotes
3. screenshot proof
4. founder-backed proof block
5. reserve / reaction snippets
