# Our Fable Messaging Rules

## One truth
The people who love your child won’t always be here.
We ask them now, so your child can hear them later.

This is the core truth to align public messaging around right now.

## What to emphasize
- the people who love your child
- asking now, while voices and stories are still here
- the child hearing or receiving this later
- private, family-first preservation
- meaningful contributions over time

## Anti-message rules
Do not lead with:
- vault
- founding families
- dispatches
- storage
- journaling
- scrapbook language
- feature lists

Do not sound like:
- a scrapbook app
- a journaling app
- a baby-tracking tool
- a generic family sharing product
- a sentimental greeting-card company

## Public-surface message hierarchy
### /reserve
Lead with:
- this is for your child
- the people who love them can leave things now
- your child receives them later
- reserve now / low friction / trust

### /gift
Lead with:
- this is a meaningful lasting gift
- the family receives a private place for their child’s story
- simpler than most people expect

### /for-your-family
Lead with:
- you’ve already seen what this becomes
- if you want the same thing for your own child, this is where to begin

## Language guardrails
Prefer:
- private family vault
- family story
- letters, voice notes, photos, and videos
- sealed for later
- the people who love them
- hear them later

Use carefully:
- legacy
- archive
- heirloom

Avoid overusing:
- platform
- capture memories
- preserve memories
- memory app
- content
- engagement

## Working test rule
If a sentence could belong on:
- a scrapbook app
- a baby journal
- a cloud-photo tool
then it is probably not specific enough for Our Fable.
